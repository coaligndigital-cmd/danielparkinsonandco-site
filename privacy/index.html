Privacy Policy

How Daniel Parkinson & Co. handles personal information.

This page explains what information may be collected through the website, how it is used and the choices available to you.

Start a conversation Back to home

Last updated: July 2026

Daniel Parkinson & Co. respects your privacy and only collects personal information where it is needed to respond to enquiries, provide services or understand how the website is used.

Who is responsible for your information?

Daniel Parkinson & Co. is responsible for the personal information submitted through this website. You can contact Daniel Parkinson & Co. through the contact form.

Information collected

When you submit an enquiry, the information collected may include:

  • Your name
  • Your email address
  • Your business or organisation
  • The service or area of interest you select
  • The message you choose to send

The website may also collect basic analytics information, such as pages visited, device type, browser type and general location data, where analytics cookies have been accepted.

How information is used

Your information may be used to:

  • Respond to enquiries
  • Provide information you have requested
  • Manage potential or existing client relationships
  • Improve the website and its content
  • Understand which pages and topics are useful to visitors

Lawful basis for processing

Enquiry information is processed because it is necessary to respond to your request and, where relevant, to take steps before entering into a contract.

Analytics information is processed with your consent where optional analytics cookies are accepted.

Third-party services

The contact form is processed using Web3Forms. Website analytics may be provided by Google Analytics. These services may process information in accordance with their own privacy practices.

No personal information is sold.

Daniel Parkinson & Co. does not sell personal information to third parties.

How long information is kept

Enquiry information is kept only for as long as needed to respond to your enquiry and manage any resulting relationship, unless a longer retention period is required for legal, accounting or legitimate business reasons.

How information is protected

Reasonable technical and organisational measures are used to protect personal information from unauthorised access, loss, misuse or disclosure.

Your rights

Depending on the circumstances, you may have the right to:

  • Ask for a copy of the personal information held about you
  • Ask for inaccurate information to be corrected
  • Ask for information to be deleted
  • Ask for processing to be restricted
  • Object to certain uses of your information
  • Withdraw consent where processing relies on consent

To exercise any of these rights, please use the contact form.

Cookies

More information about cookies and analytics is available in the Cookie Policy.

Changes to this policy

This Privacy Policy may be updated from time to time to reflect changes to the website, services or legal requirements. The latest version will always be published on this page.

Contact

For questions about this Privacy Policy or how your information is used, please use the contact form.
